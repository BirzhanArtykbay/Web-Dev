About Mountain

Mauna Kea: The tallest mountain in the world when measured from its base on the ocean floor, it stands at 13,796 feet above sea level. It's a dormant volcano with observatories at its summit.
Mauna Loa: The largest volcano on Earth by volume, rising 13,681 feet above sea level. It is an active shield volcano.
Haleakalā: Located on Maui, this massive shield volcano is known for its scenic crater and stunning sunrise views.
Kīlauea: One of the most active volcanoes in the world, located on the Big Island.
Waimea Canyon: Found on Kauai, it is nicknamed the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific."

Travel ways

Mauna Kea (Big Island)

  1. Location: Near Hilo and Kona.
  2. How to Get There:
    • Rent a 4WD vehicle; it's required for the road to the summit.
    • Drive to the Mauna Kea Visitor Information Station (at 9,200 feet) for acclimation.
    • If you're not comfortable driving, join a guided tour.
  3. Tip: Dress warmly—it can get very cold at the summit.
